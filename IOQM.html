About IOQM

The Indian Olympiad Qualifier in Mathematics (IOQM) is the prestigious first step in India's rigorous selection process for the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO). This national-level examination identifies and nurtures exceptional mathematical talent among pre-university students across the country.

Managed by Infomo Education in collaboration with official organizers, IOQM serves as the foundation for India's participation in the world's most prestigious mathematical competition for high school students.

Examination Structure

Duration: 3 hours

Question Type: 30 problems requiring numerical answers

Answer Range: Whole numbers between 00 and 99

Marking Scheme: Each correct answer typically carries equal weight

Tools Allowed: Only writing instruments, compass, and ruler (no calculators)

Syllabus

The IOQM syllabus encompasses various mathematical topics, excluding calculus. The examination tests students' understanding and application of concepts in the following key areas:

Algebra (35% weightage)

  • Polynomial equations and inequalities
  • Functional equations
  • Sequences and series (including recursive sequences)
  • Complex numbers in algebra

Number Theory (25% weightage)

  • Divisibility properties
  • Prime number theorems
  • Modular arithmetic applications
  • Diophantine equations

Geometry (25% weightage)

  • Advanced Euclidean geometry
  • Power of points and radical axes
  • Inversion and projective geometry concepts
  • Coordinate geometry transformations

Combinatorics (15% weightage)

  • Advanced counting techniques
  • Graph theory basics
  • Probabilistic methods
  • Extremal combinatorics

Selection Process

The IOQM is the first stage in a rigorous six-stage selection process to identify and train India's team for the International Mathematical Olympiad:

IOQM

Initial screening based on a 3-hour exam with 30 problems

Date: September 2025

Regional Mathematical Olympiad (RMO)

A 3-hour exam with six descriptive problems

Date: November 2025

Indian National Mathematical Olympiad (INMO)

A 4.5-hour exam with six challenging problems

Date: January 2026

IMOTC Training Camp

Intensive training camp where top performers are selected

Date: April-June 2026

Pre-Departure Camp

Final training before the IMO

International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO)

The selected team represents India at the IMO

Date: July 2026

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) IOQM

1. What is IOQM?

The Indian Olympiad Qualifier in Mathematics (IOQM) is a national-level examination that serves as the first stage in India's selection process for the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO). It assesses students' mathematical aptitude and problem-solving skills.

2. Who conducts the IOQM?

IOQM is jointly organized by the Mathematics Teachers Association of India (MTA(I)) and the Homi Bhabha Centre for Science Education (HBCSE) under the National Board for Higher Mathematics (NBHM).

3. Who is eligible to appear for IOQM?

Students from Classes 8 to 12 who are Indian citizens and born on or after August 1, 2004, are eligible to appear for IOQM. Additionally, they should not have commenced studies in a university or equivalent institution.

4. What is the format of the IOQM exam?

The IOQM is a 3-hour pen-and-paper examination consisting of 30 questions. Answers are numeric, ranging from 00 to 99, and must be marked on an OMR sheet.

5. What is the syllabus for IOQM?

The IOQM syllabus includes topics from algebra, number theory, geometry, combinatorics, and basic mathematics. Calculus is excluded.

6. How can I register for IOQM?

Registration for IOQM is typically done through the official website ioqm.in. Students can register individually or through their schools.

7. What is the fee for IOQM registration?

The registration fee for IOQM is generally nominal. The exact amount and payment details are provided during the registration process on the official website.

8. When is the IOQM exam conducted?

The IOQM exam is usually held in the first week of September each year. Specific dates are announced on the official website.

9. What happens after IOQM?

Students who perform well in IOQM qualify for the Regional Mathematical Olympiad (RMO), followed by the Indian National Mathematical Olympiad (INMO), the International Mathematical Olympiad Training Camp (IMOTC), and finally, the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO).

10. Where can I find past IOQM papers and solutions?

Previous years' IOQM question papers and answer keys are available on the official IOQM website under the 'Posts' section.
