About NSO

What is NSO?

The National Science Olympiad (NSO) is a prestigious annual competition organized by the Science Olympiad Foundation (SOF) to promote scientific thinking among school students.

Eligibility

Open to students from Class 1 to 12 from SOF-affiliated schools. There's no minimum percentage requirement.

Exam Pattern

Objective-type questions testing logical reasoning, science concepts, and higher-order thinking skills.

Frequently Asked Questions

Registration is done through your school. Contact your school coordinator to participate. Individual registrations are not accepted.

The exam consists of multiple-choice questions divided into three sections: Logical Reasoning, Science, and Achievers Section. The number of questions varies by class level.

Level 1 results are typically declared within 6-8 weeks after the exam. Level 2 results come about 4-6 weeks after that exam.

You can prepare by:

  • Studying your school science curriculum thoroughly
  • Practicing with previous year papers
  • Solving logical reasoning questions
  • Using SOF's official sample papers
